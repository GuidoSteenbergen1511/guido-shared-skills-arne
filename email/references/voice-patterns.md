# Voice Patterns

## Dutch Patterns

- **Subject-dropping:** "Heb al besproken" (drop "Ik"), "Gaan het overslaan" (drop "We")
- **"Komt goed"** = I'll handle it. Very common.
- **"Laat (maar/even) weten"** = just let me know. Signature closing phrase.
- **"Hopelijk..."** as sentence starter: "Hopelijk gaat alles goed!", "Hopelijk werkt alles nu"
- **"Mochten er vragen zijn, laat het gerust weten!"** = formulaic closing for external Dutch
- **"qua"** + noun: "qua aanpak", "qua staffing", "qua planning"
- **"Zouden jullie..."** for polite requests: "Zouden jullie dit deze week kunnen aanleveren?"
- **Dutch-English code-switching is natural:** "staffing", "scope", "tentative", "confirmen", "updaten", "go/no-go"

## English Patterns

- **"Quick heads up:"** as a transition phrase
- **"Let me know how you'd like to proceed"** to give control to recipient
- **"Happy to [verb]..."** as offer pattern
- **"I'd rather be honest with you now than overcommit and underdeliver"** for declines
- **"Looking at [topic]:"** to introduce structured info
- **"Been thinking about..." / "Just finished..."** as natural starters
- Contractions always in casual: we're, I'll, can't, wouldn't

## Structural Patterns

- **Forwarding assessment:** "Mijn inschatting:" / "Voorstel qua aanpak:" -> bullet options -> binary question
- **Status updates:** Numbered by topic, not chronological. Bold labels for sub-items.
- **Closing action requests:** End with specific, closed question: "Als jij X kunt confirmen dan Y"
- **Multi-topic emails:** One bold heading per topic, keep related detail nested under it

## Tone Calibration

| Recipient | Tone | Formality |
|---|---|---|
| Very close internal colleague | Very casual, subject-drop, no greeting | Low |
| Internal team member | Casual, "Hoi", friendly | Low-Medium |
| Internal professional (different dept.) | Direct, efficient, professional | Medium |
| External client (ongoing) | Warm, structured, no filler | Medium-High |
| C-suite / first contact | Polished, no contractions | High |
